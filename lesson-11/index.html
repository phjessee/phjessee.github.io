Preston Idaho Attractions

The Saint Maries River is located in Idaho. The river is an outlet from the Coeur d'Alene Lake, Spokane River Basin, and Columbia River Basin. The river disperses and ends near Clarkia, Idaho. The Saint Maries River is home to fishing and amazing nature. The River brings people from all over to enjoy it's beauty and to participate in recreational activities such as kayaking, tubing, fishing and many other water sports.
The run starts off with about an hour of fast moving flatwater, winding through an open canyon. The first rapid of note is marked by a string of old railroad cars buried into the river right bank, propping up the rail road bed. At the first bridge, the gradient picks up and the river drops into a an inner gorge, known as "The Loops", where the canyon forms a series of oxbows. The Loops consists of long, continuous class II and III rapids, formed by basalt ledges and sharp turns against walls. As flows increase, the Loops become faster, more continuous and more difficult. At the next railroad bridge, the river flattens out again, with about another hour of swift, flatwater to the takeout. Just past the third railroad bridge, be ready to eddy out immediately on river left. This is the take out. Due to the extremely remote nature of the canyon, continuous whitewater and cold early season weather, the run is recommended for class III paddlers only. Some sources rate the run as class II, but it in my opinion it is defintely class III. An old railroad follows the river the entire length of the run should you need to walk out. Dress warm and bring snacks, as this is a long run and it can get quite chilly in the early season.
